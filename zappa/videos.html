This is a performance of one of Frank Zappa's most famous and renowned compositions: Montana (A Token Of His Extreme). Zappa released this song on his 1973 LP Over-Nite Sensation. It is the last track on the album and features backing vocals by Tina Turner and the Ikettes. This particular recording was filmed on August 27, 1974 in Hollywood. The song is about a person moving to Montana so they can "grow a crop of dental floss" to become a "dental floss tycoon". This is my favorite Frank Zappa song.

This is a video of a portion of Zappa's guitar solo in a performance of Montana in Stockholm 1973. This clip features Zappa's improvisational genius as he shreds with the Mothers of Invention jamming along in the background. The look on George Duke's face(The keyboard player) is one of pure bliss. This is also a personal favorite of mine.

This is the music video to Inca Roads performed by Zappa and the Mothers of Invention. The surrealistic lyrics of this song explore ancient civilizations and their mystical beliefs, touching on themes of spirituality and the mysteries of the universe. This video also features claymation from the talented Bruce Bickford, a clay animation artist that worked many times with Zappa.

Watermelon in Easter Hay is one of Frank Zappa's most soulful and beautiful pieces. This performance took place on May 17th 1988 in Palace De Deportes Barcelone. Many see this song as the definitive masterpiece of Zappa's works.
